# legal-translation

**Legal document translation that understands law — not just language.**

Built by **Arjun Singh Chouhan** as the international extension of [Modern Jurist](https://github.com/braveheartjun/Modern-Jurist) — a legal translation app originally developed for the Indian legal ecosystem (English ↔ Hindi, Marathi, Gujarati, Kannada). This skill takes the same philosophy global.

---

## When to Use

- Translating any legal document between any two languages
- Producing a translation that reads as if drafted natively in the target language
- Transliterating party names, company names, or legal terms across scripts
- Creating bilingual (side-by-side) versions of contracts or clauses
- Identifying jurisdictional gaps when translating between legal systems (e.g. common law → civil law)
- Looking up correct legal term equivalents across languages and scripts

---

## How It Works

The skill operates across four layers simultaneously:

1. **Linguistic** — correct grammar, syntax, vocabulary
2. **Legal-technical** — terms of art, not dictionary translations
3. **Jurisdictional** — how this document type is actually drafted in the target legal system
4. **Pragmatic** — reads like it was drafted natively, not translated

Before translating, it identifies the document type, builds a glossary of defined terms, and maps them consistently throughout. It ends every translation with Translator's Notes flagging untranslatable concepts, jurisdictional gaps, and a certification disclaimer.

---

## Document Types Supported

Contracts, MoUs, affidavits, court orders, power of attorney, wills, lease agreements, legal notices, petitions, board resolutions, IP assignments, employment contracts, and more.

---

## Languages

All language pairs. Includes native script support and transliteration standards for:
- **South Asian:** Hindi, Marathi, Gujarati, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu, Kannada, and more
- **European:** Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, and more
- **Middle Eastern:** Arabic (MSA), Hebrew, Persian, Turkish
- **East/Southeast Asian:** Mandarin, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian
- **Other:** Russian, Ukrainian, Swahili, Amharic, and more

## Examples

**Translate an NDA into Hindi:**
> "Translate this NDA into Hindi. Preserve all clause numbering and legal conventions."

**Transliterate party names:**
> "Transliterate these party names into Devanagari for a Hindi contract: John Thompson, Fatima Al-Hassan, Zhang Wei."

**Detect jurisdictional gaps:**
> "Translate these clauses into French — flag anything that needs civil law adaptation."

**Bilingual output:**
> "Create a bilingual English/Hindi version of clauses 5 and 8, side by side."

## Limitations

- Not a substitute for a certified legal translator for court filing or official submission
- Jurisdictional conventions are based on common drafting practices — local counsel review recommended for high-stakes documents
- Transliteration is phonetic approximation — some sounds have no exact equivalent across scripts
